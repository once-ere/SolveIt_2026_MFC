Information

  • Click the right mouse button when over the Command Window to diplay a menu of examples.
    • Select an example.
    • The commands that define the problem are executed in the Command Window.
  • Do not press 'Enter' to enter a typed command; use 'Shift+Enter'.
  • Terminate text commands with a ';' [ without the quotes].
  • If the example menus are inactive then press the 'Stop' button to enable them.
  • Simulator has support for simple vector operations such as cross product, scalar product, ... .
    • x = [1,2,3];
    • y = [-4,5,6];
    • x^y; [ cross product ]
    • x*y; [ scalar product]
    • operations such as c=(x-y)^(x+2*y); ( prints [ -9 , -54 , 39 ] ) are supported.
  • Simulator accepts user defined functions as force components.
  • Analytic sphere-sphere collision detection; sphere-block, sphere-cylinder, block-block, .. collision detection is numerical.
  • Quantum Mechanics
    • Scattering in 1 Spatial Dimension
    • Energy eigenvalues in 1 and 2 Spatial Dimensions
